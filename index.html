Professional Summary

Seminars, Trainings & What I Learned

Throughout my academic journey, I have actively engaged in a series of seminars, trainings, and certification programs that have meaningfully broadened my perspective as a future IT professional. Each experience contributed not only to my technical knowledge but also to my personal and professional growth.

01
Generative AI
AI Awareness & Digital Ethics
This seminar introduced me to the rapidly evolving world of Generative Artificial Intelligence — how AI models generate text, images, code, and other content from patterns learned through training data. I explored real-world applications across industries and learned about responsible and ethical use of AI, covering topics like misinformation, deepfakes, bias in AI systems, and the importance of human oversight.
Key Takeaways
AI literacy and awareness
Understanding generative models and their applications
Digital ethics and responsible AI use
Critical evaluation of AI-generated content
02
Technology at Work
Digital Transformation & Workplace Readiness
This seminar explored how digital technologies are reshaping modern workplaces. I gained insight into how businesses leverage cloud platforms, collaboration software, and automation to drive innovation. The session highlighted the importance of being digitally fluent — not just knowing how to use tools, but understanding why and when to use them.
Key Takeaways
Digital workplace readiness
Understanding cloud and collaboration tools
Adaptability to emerging technologies
Professional competency in digital environments
03
HTML Essentials
Web Development Fundamentals — Cisco Networking Academy
Offered through the Cisco Networking Academy under the DICT-ITU DTC Initiative, this course provided a hands-on introduction to HTML — the backbone of every webpage. I learned to structure web content using semantic tags, create forms, embed images and links, and write clean, standards-compliant code.
Key Takeaways
HTML document structure and syntax
Semantic markup and accessibility
Forms, tables, links, and multimedia embedding
Web standards and clean code practices
04
Exploring Coding
Programming Logic & Problem-Solving
This seminar was an engaging introduction to programming and computational thinking. It emphasized the core logic behind writing code — breaking complex problems into manageable steps and expressing solutions using variables, conditionals, loops, and functions. The experience shifted my perspective from seeing coding as intimidating to viewing it as a creative way of thinking.
Key Takeaways
Computational thinking and problem decomposition
Basic programming constructs (variables, loops, conditionals)
Logical reasoning and algorithm design
Confidence and curiosity in coding
05
Discovering Data
Data Literacy & Foundational Analytics
I was introduced to data as one of the most valuable assets in the modern world. I learned how data is collected, stored, processed, and analyzed to generate insights that drive decisions across industries. The session covered data types, visualization techniques, and how to interpret charts and graphs critically.
Key Takeaways
Data literacy and interpretation
Basic data types and structures
Data visualization and chart reading
Foundations of data-driven decision-making
06
Emotional Intelligence
Self-Awareness, Empathy & Interpersonal Skills
This seminar focused on emotional intelligence (EQ) — the five core components: self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, and social skills. I examined how emotions influence behavior and relationships, and learned how to manage stress, communicate effectively, handle conflicts with maturity, and build stronger connections with peers.
Key Takeaways
Self-awareness and emotional regulation
Empathy and active listening
Conflict resolution and communication
Resilience and stress management
07
Critical Thinking
Analytical Reasoning & Sound Judgment
This seminar challenged me to think deeply, question assumptions, and evaluate evidence before drawing conclusions. I learned frameworks for logical reasoning, argument analysis, and identifying cognitive biases. Through case studies, I practiced applying critical thinking to real-world scenarios and making sound decisions under uncertainty.
Key Takeaways
Logical reasoning and argument analysis
Identifying and overcoming cognitive biases
Evidence-based decision making
Evaluating information sources critically
08
Lifelong Learning
Growth Mindset & Continuous Self-Development
This seminar explored the philosophy of lifelong learning — the commitment to continuously acquiring new knowledge and skills. In technology, where new tools emerge constantly, the ability to learn and adapt is essential. I learned strategies for self-directed learning, setting personal development goals, and maintaining motivation through challenges.
Key Takeaways
Self-directed learning strategies
Growth mindset and adaptability
Goal-setting and personal development planning
Resilience in the face of change and uncertainty
09
IT Career Roadmaps
Career Planning & Industry Pathways in Technology
This seminar provided a comprehensive overview of career pathways in IT — from software development and cybersecurity to data science, cloud computing, and UI/UX design. Industry professionals shared practical advice on navigating the job market, building a strong portfolio, and positioning oneself for growth. It helped me map out my own career goals clearly.
Key Takeaways
Understanding IT career pathways and specializations
Industry certifications and their value
Portfolio building and personal branding
Strategic career planning and goal setting
